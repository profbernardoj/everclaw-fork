## User Persona: Bitcoin Self-Custodian

### Demographics
- **Age:** 25-55 (primary: 30-50)
- **Gender:** Male-skewed (85%+ based on surveys)
- **Location:** Global, heavy in US, Europe, Latin America, Southeast Asia
- **Technical Skill:** Intermediate to advanced (understands wallets, exchanges)
- **Income:** Middle to high income (Bitcoin requires capital)
- **Values:** Sovereignty, privacy, decentralization, long-term thinking

### Psychographics

**Core Beliefs (from Bitcoin community research):**
1. **"Not your keys, not your coins"** — Self-custody is non-negotiable
2. **"Don't trust, verify"** — Verify everything yourself
3. **"Bitcoin is the best money"** — Superior to fiat alternatives
4. **"HODL"** — Long-term holding, not trading
5. **"Bitcoin fixes this"** — Solution to monetary debasement

**Motivations:**
- Financial sovereignty and self-reliance
- Protection from inflation and currency debasement
- Privacy from government surveillance
- Participation in monetary revolution
- Building generational wealth
- Technical curiosity and learning

**Pain Points:**
- Complexity of self-custody (hardware wallets, seed phrase security)
- Fear of losing access (death, incapacity, forgotten passwords)
- Wife/family doesn't understand Bitcoin
- Tax reporting nightmare (tracking every transaction)
- On-chain analysis paralysis (understanding mempool, fees, UTXOs)
- Phishing scams, social engineering attacks
- No trusted source for Bitcoin education (too much noise)
- Managing multiple wallets across platforms

**Aspirations:**
- True financial independence
- Secure multi-generational wealth transfer
- Privacy in financial transactions
- Understanding Bitcoin at a deep technical level
- Running a full node, contributing to network
- Helping family and friends adopt Bitcoin

### Buying Behavior
- Willing to spend $100-$500 on hardware wallets
- Willing to spend $200-$1,000 on hardware (node, RaspiBlitz, etc.)
- Highly skeptical of "crypto" projects outside Bitcoin
- Research extensively before trusting any product
- Value peer recommendations over ads
- Strong brand loyalty to "Bitcoin-only" companies

## Competitive Landscape

### What Bitcoin Holders Currently Use for AI/Management

| Solution | Pros | Cons |
|----------|------|------|
| **Hardware Wallets (Ledger, Trezor, Coldcard)** | Secure storage | No AI, limited interface, no management features |
| **Specter/Sparrow Wallet** | Advanced features, coin control | Technical learning curve, no AI |
| **Bitcoin Tax Software** | Automated reporting | Expensive ($200+/yr), no other features |
| **CoinTracker/Koinly** | Multi-chain | Expensive, not Bitcoin-focused, cloud-based (privacy concern) |
| **Lightning Nodes (RaspiBlitz, Umbrel)** | Full sovereignty | Technical, no AI assistance |
| **ChatGPT for Bitcoin Questions** | Accessible | No access to your wallet data, generic advice |
| **Bitcoin Twitter/Telegram** | Community wisdom | Noise, scams, no personalization |

### The Gap (BitcoinClaw Opportunity)

Bitcoin holders want:
1. **AI that understands their stack** — Knows their wallets, UTXOs, Lightning channels
2. **Self-custody assistance** — Helps with hardware wallet setup, inheritance planning
3. **Privacy-first** — Runs locally, no data leaves their machine
4. **On-chain intelligence** — Understands mempool, fee estimation, transaction analysis
5. **Tax automation** — Tracks cost basis, generates reports
6. **Family coordination** — Onboards spouse, sets up inheritance

**BitcoinClaw provides all of this, respecting Bitcoin values.**
